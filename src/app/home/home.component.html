FEATURES

What Py-Link does
for you

Checks your selected sources

Py-Link reads the websites and feeds you choose. It looks for new and useful updates from those sources.

Prepares content for you

After checking your sources, Py-Link writes a short post based on what it found. You do not have to write anything from scratch.

Sends results to your inbox

Your summary and post drafts are sent to your email. You can read them, make changes if needed, and then post.

Matches your style

You can set how you want the posts to sound. Py-Link will follow that style each time it prepares content for you.

Choose your time range

You can set how far back Py-Link should look. Choose 24 hours for recent news or a full week for a wider view.

Full source control

Enable or disable any source. Add your own feeds. Organise by tier, official labs, research outlets, tech media or custom.

FAQ

Common questions

Here are some answers that may help you before you start.

How does Py-Link help me save time?

Normally you would need to visit many websites, read articles, decide what is worth sharing and then write a post. Py-Link does this for you. It checks your sources, picks useful stories and prepares a short post. You just review and post. Most people save a few hours each week by using it.

Do I need to know anything technical to use Py-Link?

No. Py-Link is designed to be easy to use. You just pick your sources, set a time range, enter your email and run it. The whole setup takes about two minutes. You do not need any technical background.

Can I change how the posts sound?

Yes. You can choose the style you prefer such as professional, conversational or analytical. Py-Link will use that style each time it prepares content for you. You can always edit the drafts before posting.

Where do I receive my results?

Py-Link sends the results to your email. You will get a short summary and ready-to-use post drafts. Each draft includes the original article link so you can check the source before posting. You do not need to log in to see the results.

Which sources can Py-Link check?

Py-Link comes with a set of ready-to-use feeds. You can also add your own sources using any RSS feed or website URL. You can turn sources on or off anytime depending on what is useful to you.

Which platforms does Py-Link support?

Py-Link currently supports LinkedIn and Twitter/X. It prepares each post to match the format of that platform. More platforms are being added. You can choose which platform to use before each run.

What if I am not happy with a draft?

You are always in control. You can edit any draft, rewrite it or ignore it. Nothing is posted without your action. If you want better results, you can change your sources or tone settings and run Py-Link again.
